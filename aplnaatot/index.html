Hosts

Rich Park is an APL instructor at Dyalog Ltd, has taught APL at multiple schools, and created an online self-study course.

Adám Brudzewsky founded the APL Orchard chat room and APLcart, and is the co-author of the APL Cultivations book.

Both are Dyalog Ltd employees, regular hosts of Dyalog webinars, and recurring panellists on the Array Cast podcast.

2022-11-01: Notational Awkwardnesses
 In this episode of the APL Notation As a Tool Of Thought (APLNAATOT) podcast, Richard and Adám discuss the evolution of the 2D Iverson Notation into modern APL, alternative syntaxes, and also notational awkwardnesses stemming from binding strengths; stranding, bracket axis and indexing, issues with the rank operator, etc.
2022-10-06: Indexing in APL vs Yorick
 In this inaugural episode, we discuss the indexing notation and broadcasting rules of the Yorick programming language based on a tweet by Jeremy Howard, and conclude that the two approaches achieve the same thing; one by manipulating data shape until broadcasting achieves desired result, the other by applying additional broadcasting rules on the existing shapes.
